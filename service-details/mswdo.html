About Protective Services

Provides immediate intervention and support for individuals and families experiencing crisis situations including abuse, neglect, exploitation, and other difficult circumstances.

Beneficiaries

  • Victims of abuse, neglect, and exploitation
  • Children in conflict with the law (CICL)
  • Women in especially difficult circumstances
  • Abandoned and neglected children
  • Families in crisis situations
  • Victims of trafficking

Services Provided

Crisis Intervention

Immediate response and intervention for emergency cases.

Temporary Shelter

Safe shelter for victims needing immediate protection.

Counseling Services

Psychosocial support and counseling for affected individuals.

Legal Assistance

Referral and coordination for legal aid services.

About the Feeding Program

The Supplementary Feeding Program aims to improve the nutritional status of malnourished children aged 0-5 years old through provision of nutritious meals and nutrition education.

Program Components

Hot Meals

Nutritious hot meals provided daily to beneficiaries.

Growth Monitoring

Regular weighing and height measurement to track progress.

Nutrition Education

Parent education on proper nutrition and child care.

Requirements

Birth Certificate
Barangay Certificate
Nutritional Assessment
Parent/Guardian ID

About Day Care Services

The Day Care Service Program provides early childhood care and development (ECCD) services to children aged 3-4 years old, preparing them for formal schooling while their parents work.

Socialization

Health Monitoring

Regular health check-ups and growth monitoring.

Feeding

Nutritious snacks and meals during day care hours.

About SEA-K Program

The Self-Employment Assistance - Kaunlaran (SEA-K) program provides capital assistance to disadvantaged individuals and families to start or expand small-scale livelihood projects.

Eligible Beneficiaries

  • Low-income families and individuals
  • Unemployed or underemployed residents
  • Solo parents
  • Persons with disabilities (PWDs)
  • Senior citizens with viable projects

About Social Pension

The Social Pension Program provides monthly stipend to indigent senior citizens aged 60 years old and above to help augment their daily subsistence and medical needs.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Filipino citizen, 60 years old and above
  • Frail, sickly, or with disability
  • No pension from government or private agencies
  • No permanent source of income
  • No regular support from family

Requirements for Application

Senior Citizen ID
Birth Certificate/Baptismal
Certificate of Indigency
Barangay Certificate
1x1 ID Photo
Medical Certificate (if applicable)

About SLP

The Sustainable Livelihood Program is a community-based capacity building program that provides participants with training, technical and financial assistance to improve their socio-economic status.

Program Tracks

Microenterprise Development (MD)

For participants who want to start or expand their own business. Includes seed capital fund and business development training.

Employment Facilitation (EF)

For participants seeking employment. Includes skills training, job matching, and employment assistance.

Requirements

4Ps ID/Certificate
Valid Government ID
Barangay Certificate
Business Proposal (for MD)

About AICS

AICS provides emergency assistance to individuals and families in crisis situations including medical, burial, educational, and transportation assistance.

Types of Assistance

Medical Assistance

Financial aid for hospitalization, medicines, laboratory tests, and medical procedures.

Burial Assistance

Financial support for funeral and burial expenses of indigent families.

Educational Assistance

Support for school fees, supplies, and other educational needs.

Transportation Assistance

Travel assistance for medical referrals and emergency situations.

General Requirements

Request Letter
Certificate of Indigency
Valid ID
Barangay Certificate
Supporting Documents
Medical Abstract (for medical)
